# Buffalo Soldier — Context Brief
*Movement I of Organic Memory (Triptych)*

> See `00_triptych-overview.md` for shared framework, motif, and hardware inventory.

## Conceptual Premise
Explores the co-commodification of Black bodies and the American bison under colonial expansion. Speculative narrative: a Buffalo Soldier and an American buffalo meet and recognize each other — both nearly driven to extinction, both carrying the weight of genocidal logic. The name "Buffalo Soldier" itself binds Black bodies to bison through colonial violence (given by Indigenous peoples, referencing either fierce fighting or hair texture resembling buffalo fur).

Refuses positioning Buffalo Soldiers as simple heroes or villains. Locates them within the larger machinery of extraction and elimination.

## Material / Sonic Identity
**Primary interaction:** Tactile — participants touch, strike, manipulate earth materials in wooden substrate boxes. Vibrations captured by piezoelectric sensors and contact mics.

**Physical objects:** Wooden boxes (multiple, different sizes) containing rocks, soil, sand, clay. Sensors buried/embedded. Wires visible but organized (critical technical practice ethos — apparatus legibility as Intimate Witnessing).

**Sonic character:** Ambiguous — hard to distinguish stone from organic. Blur between human/animal, earth/body. Gritty, granular, heavy. Refuses clean reverb or concert-hall acoustics.

**Temporal character:** Geological. Extremely slow. One "beat" of the motif every 4–8 seconds.

**Spatialization:** 8-channel minimum, ambisonics preferred. Two spatial layers:
- Layer A (Bison/Earth): slow circular rotation, grazing arcs. Low frequencies, dense textures. Movement data from bison range maps 1720–1889.
- Layer B (Soldier/March): interrupted linear trajectories cutting across field. Percussive attacks. Movement data from Buffalo Soldier fort/camp locations and campaign routes.
- These two layers interact, interrupt, and occasionally merge.

## Motif Expression
The [triplet + short-short-short-short-LONG] cell stretched to geological time:
- Triplet pickup → three slow subsonic pulses (felt, not heard as pitch)
- Four shorts → four distinct material/tactile events in space
- Long note → sustained granular cloud, material settling

## ST Domain Integration
- **FS:** Refuses hero/villain binary; speculates on shared memory between soldier and bison
- **MM:** Earth materials carry sedimented memory; tactile interaction grounds work in embodied gesture referencing burial, migration, confinement
- **SW:** Must touch to generate sound; must move through space for spatialization; multisensory
- **IW:** Effectiveness depends on whether granular fragmentation "feels like" rupture; requires witnesses who can recognize cultural resonance
