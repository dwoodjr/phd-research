# Did Sun Ra Fear Cryosleep? — Context Brief
*Movement II of Organic Memory (Triptych)*

> See `00_triptych-overview.md` for shared framework, motif, and hardware inventory.

## Conceptual Premise
Sun Ra claimed to have arrived on Earth from Saturn (Space is the Place, 1974). The work asks: on his journey, did he fear entering cryosleep? This opens onto how Black bodies are depicted in states of suspension and passage through space-time in science fiction — amniotic pods, cryo-chambers, stasis fields — and how these visual tropes carry the historical resonance of the Middle Passage: bodies packed into ship holds, the Atlantic as grave and archive. Water and fluid become sites where historical fact and speculative fiction converge.

## Material / Sonic Identity
**Primary interaction:** Ritual — participants submerge symbolic artifacts into water vessels. The artifacts' dissolution (fizzing, crackling, sinking, floating, fragmenting) is captured by hydrophones.

**Physical objects:** Array of transparent glass vessels (cryopod array) — cylindrical, ovoid, varied sizes — on a dark matte surface. Each vessel = one "pod," one passenger, one hydrophone channel. Vessels at varied heights (reinforcing vertical spatialization physically). Subtle underlighting (deep blue/bioluminescent palette).

**Dissolving artifacts:** Salt tablets, sugar cubes, water-soluble paper, effervescent tablets, wax-cast objects with embedded symbolic materials (soil, seeds, hair, thread). Some dissolve rapidly (seconds), some slowly (minutes). Dissolution rate becomes compositional parameter.

**Sonic character:** Unstable, transitional. "Not yet arrived, not fully dissolved." Roughness and instability amplified, not smoothed. Earthly dissolution + cosmic travel coexisting. Refuses spa-like water aesthetics.

**Temporal character:** Chemical — determined by material dissolution rate, not metronome. Extremely slow. This is the longest-duration movement.

**Spatialization:** Vertical — sounds have "weight" and "density." Heavy/low frequencies settle downward. Light/high/granular sounds float upward. Individual hydrophone channels mix dynamically. Possible additional sensors: conductivity (tracks dissolved solids in real-time), turbidity, temperature.

## Motif Expression
The cell dissolved and reconstituted by chemistry:
- Triplet pickup → three artifact submersion events in sequence
- Four shorts → four phases of dissolution (rapid → slowing → near-stillness → complete)
- Long note → silence of complete dissolution; only sediment remains

Tempo is material-dependent. A sugar cube might complete the cell in 30 seconds. Wax might take 10 minutes. The material sets the tempo — organic music logic taken literally.

## Somax2 Corpus
- NASA electromagnetic recordings (Voyager, solar wind, pulsar data) — NASA SoundCloud, free
- Water-imagery spirituals ("Wade in the Water," "Deep River," "Go Down Moses") — Smithsonian Folkways, Library of Congress
- Ocean/ship ambient recordings — Freesound.org (Creative Commons)

When an artifact completes its dissolution cycle, Somax2 generates a response from the cosmic corpus. Human submerges → material dissolves → cosmos responds.

## ST Domain Integration
- **FS:** Refuses separation of Middle Passage trauma from speculative cosmic futures; centers Black subjectivity in cryosleep/stasis narratives that typically erase it
- **MM:** Water as historical archive (Atlantic) and material substrate; ritual of submersion engages embodied memory of baptism, burial at sea
- **SW:** Vertical spatialization demands different listening mode; earthly and cosmic scales interpenetrate
- **IW:** Ritual framing positions participants as witnesses, not users; effectiveness depends on whether sonic transformations evoke phenomenology of passage and suspension

## DIY Hydrophone Option
Basic hydrophone can be DIY-built for ~$20–30: piezo disc (50mm) sealed in epoxy or plumber's putty inside a waterproof housing, shielded cable, XLR termination. Sacrifices some sensitivity vs. commercial units but functional for development and prototyping. Worth building 1–2 for early experiments while commercial units are sourced.
