# Research Memo: Cryosleep, Colonial Ships, and the Refusal to Sleep

*Captured from a working session — raw thinking, not yet formalized.*

---

## The Core Tension

Western sci-fi's colonial/colony ships share the same underlying logic: a body of people moving through space-time toward a place they will claim and remake in their own image. The **colonial ship** did this through overt violence and extraction. The **colony ship** fantasizes about doing it through technology, progress, and survival — but the structure of departure and arrival, of transit and conquest, remains intact.

**Cryosleep** is the technological solution to the problem of the body in transit: preserve it, suspend it, deliver it intact so it can resume the project on the other side. The body doesn't change in cryosleep. That's the point. It arrives as it left — ready to colonize.

---

## Sun Ra's Refusal

Sun Ra's cosmological proposition was genuinely spatial — not metaphorical. Space was the only place where Blackness could exist outside the conditions that made Earth uninhabitable. His escape *was* the liberation. Not integration, not reform — departure. Refusal of the existing world rather than a demand that it change.

But Sun Ra's ships don't arrive anywhere. They **orbit**. They **transmit**. The escape is the point, not the destination.

He would not have slept. Staying awake the whole way is the point — remaining in process, in transformation, in the middle of the crossing. Cryosleep preserves the self that colonial logic requires: stable, intact, continuous, ready to claim.

**The dissolution in this work may be the answer to the title question.** The substrate doesn't preserve. It goes. It changes in transit. It becomes something else on the way. That is not cryosleep. That is the opposite of cryosleep.

---

## The Title as Research Question

*Did Sun Ra Fear Cryosleep?* is asking: did he fear the **preservation of the self** that colonial logic requires?

The dissolved substrate, listened to through the hydrophone, processed through an AI that has its own priors — this is a body in transit that refuses to arrive intact. The material has already decided what it is going to do. The system can only listen and compromise.

## Connection to Dissertation Framework

This connects to the enacting methods framework (DJ's dissertation work):

- **Embodying → Agential Cut → Production**: the body enters the apparatus; the cut is made through contact; something is produced that didn't exist before
- **Remixing → Boundary Work → Stabilization**: the labor of negotiating with the material in transit; the EQ, the AI, the sensors are all doing boundary work on a thing that won't stay still
- **Transmitting → Boundary Object → Circulation**: the finished work transmits across communities (Afrofuturist, sonic, technical, institutional) without being owned by any of them

Eros runs underneath all three: *whose* body makes the cut, *whose* labor does the boundary work, *whose* communities the object circulates into.

## Open Questions for Dissertation

- How does the refusal-to-arrive structure of Sun Ra's cosmology map onto the research-creation methodology? Is the work itself meant to orbit rather than land?
- What does it mean for an Afrofuturist sonic sculpture to circulate in white institutional spaces (galleries, universities, conferences) — is that landing, or is it still in transit?
- The dissolved substrate goes. The recording remains. Is the recording a form of cryosleep? What is being preserved, and for whom?
