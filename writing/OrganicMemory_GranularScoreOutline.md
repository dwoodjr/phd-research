## COMPOSER'S NOTE: Sound Before the Instrument *(\~15–20 pages)*

**1. The Drone**

- Opens *in sound* — not in argument, not in context-setting
- Introduces sonic virtuality (Grimshaw & Garner) as the condition of possibility: sound as a field of potential that exists before any instrument conditions it
- Sound is always already here — before the piezo is embedded, before the hydrophone is submerged, before the algorithm tries and fails to see hair
- This is the drone: the perceptual constant that will hum through all three movements, sometimes subliminal, sometimes audible, never absent
- Establishes the reader's ears before anything else

**2. Who Is Making This**

- Positionality: Black scholar-practitioner, specific cultural grounding, not despite but *because of* this specificity
- Why specificity is generative rather than limiting — the "specific-to-universal" move: begin in one place deeply enough and you find principles that travel
- Wynter's "ceremony found": the autopoetic act of re-envisioning the human beyond Western epistemic closure — this dissertation is a sonic ceremony finding
- Brief orientation to the three instruments and what they are — just enough to have a map

**3. A Trombone in Third Position**

- The origin story: the band director, the Yamaha vs. the Bach, the specific sharpness of third position
- The epistemological lesson learned in high school: instruments carry the intentions and sonic ideals of their makers
- "Every device, from a trombone to a software synthesizer, expresses a particular worldview about what counts as correct, beautiful, or possible"
- This is the first sounding of the leitmotif — the drone surfaces into consciousness here, then goes back under
- The sentence that opens everything: *To modify a tool is to engage in an epistemic act*

**4. Making the Score: On the Structure of This Dissertation**

- The organizing metaphor: making an instrument — not vessel/voyage, but the act of construction itself
- Sound is both tool AND material, instrument AND subject — it is the before and the after
- Two temporalities named explicitly (polytonality): geological/elemental (earth → water → body) AND historical/linear (past → speculative future → present). Both true simultaneously.
- Brief sketch of the ST framework — URET cycle and four domains, named and described in one or two sentences each. Just enough to have ears. Not exhaustive.
- Julius Eastman's *Femenine* named as structural model: the drone (perceptual constant) + the developmental cell (ST, accumulating and transforming across movements). The reader is now oriented to what the score is doing architecturally.

**5. How to Hear This** *(reading guide)*

- Names the three movements and their tempos: geological (Ground), chemical (Hold), human (Touch)
- Describes the arc: subliminal → emerging → explicit; felt → witnessed → participated
- Notes that global theories appear in service of specific material throughout, summoned by the material rather than front-loaded
- Notes that the Coda names what was discovered — MSC, the transmissible knowledge — rather than what was predetermined
- Invites the reader to listen, not just to read

## FIRST MOVEMENT: GROUND *(\~40–50 pages)*

*Buffalo Soldier — earth, rock, substrate, piezo, tactility*
*Tempo: Geological. Form: Excavation log — sections go deeper, not forward.*
*ST Domain foregrounded: Material Memory*
*Motif recognition: Subliminal. Listener relationship: Felt in the body.*

---

**Surface: The Desire and the Unsettling**

- What brought this work into being: the father's Buffalo Soldier paintings, the Colt revolver replica, the cavalry sword — objects that carried history into a childhood home
- The name itself: "Buffalo Soldier" — a name given by Indigenous peoples, referencing either fierce fighting or the texture of their hair resembling buffalo fur. Both readings bind Black bodies to the American buffalo through colonial logic.
- *Nick Estes — Our History Is the Future* enters here: the deliberate near-extinction of the American bison as a colonial weapon against Indigenous nations. The name triangulates three bodies — Black soldiers, Indigenous peoples, the buffalo — all subject to the same extractive logic.
- First encounter with the substrate material: touching earth, rock, soil — what activates in the body
- The site of unsettling: default reverb and spatialization algorithms assume neutral acoustic space. Space in these tools has no memory, no weight, no history. What would it mean to build an instrument whose space carries memory?

**First Layer: The Ground as Archive**

- *Katherine McKittrick — Demonic Grounds*: the substrate is contested ground. Black geographies as a framework for reading the relationship between Black bodies and land/earth. The earth is not neutral terrain — it has been demarcated, extracted from, walked over, buried in.
- The choice of materials: earth, rock, soil — not arbitrary substrate but specific racialized terrain. The wooden box as simultaneously the earth that holds memory AND the container that confines bodies (coffin, cargo hold, reservation boundary).
- The piezo as a listener: what does the earth say when asked with vibration? The sensor does not impose — it receives. But what it receives, and how it translates, is designed.
- *Jane Bennett — Vibrant Matter*: rocks and soil are not passive materials awaiting human activation. They have their own agency, their own force — thing-power. The piezo picks up this thing-power, not just human gesture. The material is co-authoring the sound. This is not metaphor — it is the physics of the instrument.

**Second Layer: The Encounter That Has No Archive**

- *Saidiya Hartman — Critical Fabulation (Venus in Two Acts)* : the speculative encounter at the heart of this work. A Buffalo Soldier meets an American buffalo. They recognize each other — both commodified, both nearly driven to extinction, both carrying the weight of the same genocidal logic enacted upon their bodies. This encounter has no archive because the archive was never built to hold it.
- The work IS the fabulation made material: the sonic sculpture enacts what cannot be documented. This is not retroactive artistic decoration — it is the research method.
- How granular synthesis enacts the fabulation: sounds torn apart and slowly reconstituted, mimicking the violence of extraction and the slow work of memory. The processing chain is the argument.
- The spatialization as migration data: the spatial logic of the work follows forced march routes, buffalo migration patterns, data extracted from historical movement records. Space is not neutral — it is the map of what was done to these bodies.

**Third Layer: The Right to Not Be Legible**

- *Édouard Glissant — Poetics of Relation*: opacity as a right. The right to not be fully legible, to not be reduced to what the dominant system can see and categorize. Both the Buffalo Soldier and the buffalo were rendered legible only as functions of colonial utility.
- The sonic ambiguity of the substrate work: you cannot tell stone from flesh from soil. This ambiguity is not a failure of the instrument — it IS the instrument's argument. The refusal to resolve into legibility.
- The piezo's democratic listening: it picks up everything without hierarchical discrimination. This is both its strength and its political stance.
- *Post-Phenomenology (Ihde)*  enters here specifically: the piezo as a hermeneutic relation — we read the world through it, but what it reads is always partial, always shaped by its own design history. Modifying that design is an epistemic act. The non-neutrality thesis applied to the specific instrument being built.

**Fourth Layer: The Instrument Built from This**

- Technical description of the work as research instrument: substrate boxes (materials, construction), piezo wiring and sensitivity calibration, electret microphone placement, Max/MSP patch architecture (signal routing, filtering, granular synthesis chain), spatialization system (multi-channel, migration-data-informed)

- *Agential Realism (Barad)* : the substrate box as apparatus. The apparatus doesn't just measure what's already there — it produces the phenomenon through intra-action. Earth + piezo + cultural encoding of the processing chain = a sound that carries colonial history. The apparatus makes this sound possible; without its specific design, this sound cannot exist.

- ST Cycle applied to this instrument:
  - *Unsettling*: default reverb/spatialization, neutral acoustic space
  - *Remixing*: modified granular chain, migration-data-informed spatialization
  - *Embodying*: does the granular fragmentation FEEL like rupture and sedimentation? Does the spatial movement feel like forced migration?
  - *Transmitting*: can another practitioner use this approach? What needs to be documented?

- ST Domains in this instrument:
  - *Fugitive Speculation*: refuses the binary hero/villain reading; imagines the recognition across colonial separation
  - *Material Memory*: the earth materials carry sedimented cultural memory; tactile interaction grounds the work in embodied gesture
  - *Sensory Worlding*: multisensory — touch generates sound; movement through space generates spatialization; the full body is the interface
  - *Intimate Witnessing*: can participants sense the historical weight? Does the granular fragmentation "feel like" rupture? This cannot be validated by technical metrics.

- What remains unresolved; productive failures; what the next ST cycle would pursue

**Deep Layer: What Ground Hears**

- Synthesis of the movement: what this instrument reveals about the practice of making
- The leitmotif ( *When Johnny Comes Marching Home*, stretched to geological time): the cell is here, at \~1 beat per 4–8 seconds, subsonic — felt in the body, not consciously heard. This is the motif at its most latent.
- The Somax2 corpus: field hollers, work songs, bugle calls. What the AI is trained to recognize and what it finds (or doesn't find) in the earth material sounds.
- The sleigh bells / drone: subsonic rumble beneath everything, tuned to the motif's long note. Never fully absent.
- Closes with a question that opens the next movement: *if the ground holds this memory — if earth is archive — what does water carry? What happens when the body enters the ocean?*

## SECOND MOVEMENT: HOLD *(\~40–50 pages)*

*Did Sun Ra Fear Cryosleep? — water, hydrophone, dissolution, suspension*
*Tempo: Chemical. Form: Ship's log / dissolution record — entries track transformation; sections that don't resolve.*
*ST Domain foregrounded: Fugitive Speculation*
*Motif recognition: Emerging. Listener relationship: Witnessed.*

---

**Entry: The Question**

- The question itself as methodology. "Did Sun Ra fear cryosleep?" — playful and serious simultaneously. A speculative question as a research instrument: it opens rather than closes.
- Sun Ra's mythology: claimed origin from Saturn, elaborated in *Space Is the Place* (1974). The interstellar traveler as a frame for asking what Black bodies in suspension must feel.
- The visual motif: science fiction cryosleep imagery — amniotic pods, stasis chambers, suspended bodies in fluid. *Foundation, Alien, Aliens, Interstellar* — and the specific additional weight this imagery carries for Black bodies.
- The convergence that this work inhabits: the Middle Passage and cryosleep share an architectural logic. Both involve Black bodies suspended in fluid, in transit, across an impossible distance of space-time. The ocean and the cryo-pod are the same container.

**Log Entry 1: The Wake**

- *Christina Sharpe — In the Wake: On Blackness and Being*: the ship, the hold, the wake, the weather. The Atlantic as both grave and archive. Wake work as methodology: to tend to the dead while the living go on living.
- The hydrophone as the instrument of wake work: it is literally listening *in the wake* — in the sonic residue, the disturbance left by bodies that passed through water. The hydrophone was never designed for this. Its cultural modification is its purpose here.
- The hold: the ship's hold and the cryo-pod's stasis chamber share the same architectural logic — containment during passage. What does the hold sound like? The hydrophone is trying to answer this.
- *Post-phenomenology (Ihde)* : the hydrophone as a hermeneutic relation. We read the ocean through it. What it makes legible is shaped by what it was designed to detect — and by what we encode in its processing.

**Log Entry 2: What the Water Holds**

- *M. NourbeSe Philip — Zong!* : the legal document of 150 enslaved Africans thrown overboard, fragmented into sound and silence. Philip does with language what the hydrophone does with water: makes the Atlantic archive audible by fragmenting and drowning the dominant narrative.
- Formal parallel: Philip fragments text; this work fragments signal. The processed hydrophone recordings refuse smooth, clean sound — the default processing that "cleans" underwater recordings is the unsettling. The modification amplifies roughness and instability as sonic metaphor for bodies in transition, not yet arrived, not fully dissolved.
- *Astrida Neimanis — Bodies of Water*: we are not bodies IN water; we are bodies OF water. Trans-corporeality — the body is continuous with the ocean. The dissolution of artifacts in the vessel is also a kind of homecoming. Dissolution is not only loss; it is also return.
- The ritual of submerging artifacts: this is not input, it is ceremony. Participants choose what to submerge, watch it dissolve or resist dissolution, witness transformation. Compositional chemistry: salt, sugar, wax, clay — each with its own dissolution rate, its own sonic character, its own symbolic resonance.

**Log Entry 3: Temporal Escape**

- *Rasheedah Phillips / Black Quantum Futurism*: Black non-linear temporality. Cryosleep is a temporal technology — you leave one time and arrive in another, bypassing the colonial timeline. Sun Ra's entire mythology is precisely this: temporal escape as liberation strategy.
- The question "did he fear it?" reinserts Black subjectivity into the technology. Fear, willingness, choice — terms that the Middle Passage systemically denied. The speculative question gives them back.
- *Octavia Butler — Xenogenesis / Lilith's Brood*: Lilith wakes from suspension into an alien ship. The Black body transformed by what preserved and violated it simultaneously. The speculative fiction that provides the narrative architecture for this movement.
- NASA sonification samples mixed with hydrophone recordings: cosmic electromagnetic waves mixed with the sounds of earthly dissolution. The Atlantic and the cosmos as the same space of Black passage, the same question of what the body carries across impossible distance.

**Log Entry 4: The Instrument Built from This**

- Technical description: hydrophone construction/sourcing, the vessel design, artifact taxonomy (dissolution rates, sonic characters, symbolic resonances), Max patch for spectral analysis and granular synthesis, vertical spatialization system (heavier sounds sink, lighter sounds float — spatial organization by fluid dynamics, not terrestrial norms)
- *Agential Realism (Barad)* : the water vessel as apparatus. The apparatus transforms — the phenomenon (the sound of dissolution) emerges through the intra-action of water + artifact + hydrophone + the cultural logic encoded in the processing chain. Remove any element and the phenomenon ceases.
- Conductivity sensor (planned): measuring what dissolved material changes in the water — transformation made measurable, the chemistry of dissolution as compositional parameter
- ST Cycle applied: *Unsettling* (default processing smooths and cleans — refuses transformation), *Remixing* (amplifying roughness, vertical spatialization), *Embodying* (does the sound feel like suspension? Like bodies in transit?), *Transmitting*
- ST Domains in this instrument:
  - *Fugitive Speculation*: refuses separation of historical trauma (Middle Passage) and speculative future (interstellar travel); centers Black subjectivity in the question
  - *Material Memory*: water as archive; the ritual of submersion engages embodied memory of baptism, burial at sea, passage
  - *Sensory Worlding*: vertical spatialization demands a different mode of listening — attuned to gradual transformation and spatial weight, not horizontal movement
  - *Intimate Witnessing*: participants as witnesses, not users; the effectiveness of the work depends on whether the sonic transformations evoke the phenomenology of suspension and passage

**Dissolution: What Holds Cannot Be Held**

- Synthesis of the movement: what this instrument reveals
- The leitmotif: determined by chemistry of dissolution, not metronome. Duration variable — the material's rhythm, not the composer's. The motif is emerging here, recognizable in outline if not in full form.
- The Somax2 corpus: water-imagery spirituals, NASA sonifications. What the AI finds in the dissolution sounds.
- The drone: vessel resonance — the specific frequency of the water container, always present beneath the dissolution sounds
- Closes with the question that opens the next movement: *if water carries the body through time — if dissolution is also transformation — what does the body itself carry? What knowledge lives in the flesh that no algorithm was trained to find?*

## THIRD MOVEMENT: TOUCH *(\~40–50 pages)*

*Don't Play with My Hair — hair, gesture, computer vision, wavetable synthesis*
*Tempo: Human. Form: Technical manual that keeps interrupting itself.*
*ST Domain foregrounded: Sensory Worlding*
*Motif recognition: Explicit. Listener relationship: Participated.*

---

**Before the Manual Begins: The Refusal and the Instrument**

- The lived experience: hands reaching toward the head without permission, the phrase "don't play with my hair" as a refusal that also names. The dual experience — violation from others, intimate self-knowledge through self-touch.
- The speculative proposition stated plainly: *what if hair could be an instrument?*  Not as metaphor — literally. This is the question that makes the HairSynth. The speculative question as design brief.
- Hair as site: simultaneously personal/political, intimate/public. The CROWN Act, workplace discrimination, school dress codes — the institutional machinery. AND: the barbershop, the salon, intergenerational hair care knowledge, natural hair movements. Both exist in the same material.

**Section 1.0: The Body as Signifying Practice**

- *Kobena Mercer — "Black Hair/Style Politics"* : foundational. Hair as a signifying practice — aesthetics and politics are inseparable in Black hair because dominant culture made them so. The HairSynth takes this signifying practice and makes it literally generative: the cultural meaning encoded in hair becomes computational input. The sign generates sound.
- *Hortense Spillers — Mama's Baby, Papa's Maybe*: the distinction between body and flesh. The flesh is what gets marked, inscribed, claimed before it becomes a social body — it is the site of racial inscription. The uninvited touch is a claim on the flesh. The HairSynth reclaims the flesh as an instrument on its own terms — generating knowledge and sound, rather than absorbing the claims of others.
- Political context: the CROWN Act and its legislative history; the long record of Black hair as a site of institutional control. This is the cultural-political apparatus the instrument is built against and from.

**Section 2.0: The Pipeline (Or: What the Algorithm Cannot See)**

- Technical description of the HairSynth pipeline v1: CT scan of curly fro → Houdini VDB → point cloud extraction → Python script → WAV files (1024 samples each) → PlugData wavetable synthesizer. Step by step, specific.
- Technical description of v2: vvvv + OpenCV → contour data, keypoints, blobs from animated video → Max/MSP parameter mapping
- *Joy Buolamwini — Gender Shades / Algorithmic Justice League*: CV systems fail to detect Black hair because their training data encoded whiteness as the norm. The algorithm literally cannot see what it was never trained to see. Type 4 hair is structurally absent from most datasets. This is not a bug — it is the argument of the algorithm.
- The unsettling as research site: the technical friction of making a CV system see Black hair is not an obstacle to the research — it IS the research. The place where the tool's embedded assumptions become visible.
- The modification: repurposing standard CV algorithms to prioritize textural analysis and edge detection at scales appropriate for hair structure. Making the algorithm see differently — a modification that is also an argument.

*\[INTERRUPTION — the manual pauses here]*
*A passage in a different register: the specific texture of touching my own hair. The sound of clippers at the back of the neck. The way you know your own hair by feel, in the dark, without looking. This is the data the pipeline is trying to extract. The body already has it. It has always had it. The pipeline is not discovering this knowledge — it is learning to ask the body a question the body has always been able to answer.*

**Section 3.0: Haptic Epistemology**

- *Laura Marks — The Skin of the Film*: haptic visuality — the skin as an epistemological organ; touch as a way of knowing that exceeds and precedes vision. The HairSynth is a haptic epistemology instrument: it makes tactile knowledge into audible form. What the fingers know becomes what the ears hear.
- The data-sound mappings: not "more texture = louder." The mappings encode embodied knowledge of what it feels like to touch hair — the specific relationships between texture parameters and synthesis parameters emerged through embodied experimentation, adjusting while listening until the output "felt right." This is not taste; it is methodology.
- *Agential Realism (Barad)* : the HairSynth as apparatus. The hair data and the synthesis system intra-act to produce a phenomenon — the sound of hair — that neither could produce alone. The cultural encoding in the mapping system is part of the apparatus.
- *Sonic virtuality (Grimshaw & Garner)* : the drone returns explicitly here. Sound was the condition of possibility before any of this was built. The HairSynth is the most intimate instantiation of that original potential.

**Section 4.0: Braiding**

- *Robin Wall Kimmerer — Braiding Sweetgrass*: with explicit care and acknowledgment of distinct traditions. Braiding as a practice of attending to material with patience, relationship, and intergenerational knowledge — as a knowledge form, not a technique. The formal resonance between tending sweetgrass and tending hair: both require the same quality of attention; both are forms of knowledge transmission. These are distinct traditions in conversation, not collapsed into one another — named explicitly as such in the text.
- The gesture recognition component: the intimate gestures — fingers running through hair, twisting strands, the self-soothing pull. These are the gestures the HairSynth wants to capture. Capacitive touch sensors (planned), proximity sensing, real-time CV refinement.
- Consent as design problem — and as unresolved question: who touches whose hair? With what permission? How do you design consent mechanisms that are not performative? The work raises this without fully resolving it, and that irresolution is part of the argument — it is the work's own site of productive failure.

**Section 5.0: The Instrument Built from This**

- Full technical documentation: both HairSynth versions, the wavetable architecture in full, vvvv contour extraction pipeline, the mapping system, the visual feedback component (hair data visualization), planned sensor integration
- The Somax2 corpus: barbershop ambience, hair care oral tradition. What the AI listens for and finds in the hair data. When alignment occurs: the motif recognizable.
- ST Cycle applied to this instrument: the complete documentation of Unsettling → Remixing → Embodying → Transmitting for the HairSynth
- ST Domains: specific application of FS, MM, SW, IW to this instrument
- What remains in development; what productive failures the next cycle will pursue

*\[FINAL INTERRUPTION — the manual cannot hold its form]*
*The moment when the output sounds right. When the wavetable synthesis produces something that resonates — not just with the data, but with the experience of having this hair, growing up with this hair, having it touched without permission and touching it yourself in the mirror. This cannot be written as a specification in the manual. It is known through the body. It is the kind of knowledge that this entire dissertation has been trying to make transmissible — and here, at the end of the third movement, it is simply present. The sound is right. You know it when you hear it.*

**Explicit: What Touch Knows**

- Synthesis of the movement: what this instrument reveals about the practice of making
- The leitmotif in its most recognizable form: human-scale, natural gesture rhythm, the triplet anacrusis + \[short-short-short-short-LONG] now audible as itself. The reader can hear what was subliminal in Ground and emerging in Hold. The developmental cell has arrived.
- The arc completed: the motif has moved from geological time (felt, not heard) through chemical time (emerging, variable) to human time (explicit, participable). The listener has been brought from feeling to witnessing to participating. This is the arc of the score.

## CODA: TRANSMISSION *(\~20–25 pages)*

*What the three instruments played together / what remains*

**The Ensemble Sound**

- What happens when Ground + Hold + Touch are heard simultaneously — as an installation, as an argument, as a methodology

- Cross-instrument design principles that emerged across all three:
  - Material as primary compositional input (not illustration, not metaphor — the material is the argument)
  - The body as data source and as evaluative criterion (the body knows before the algorithm does)
  - Modification as the epistemological act (to change the tool is to change what can be known)
  - Cultural specificity as generative constraint (the particular opens to the general; the specific-to-universal move)
  - Failure as site (every place the dominant tool could not see became a place to build something new)

- The shared motif named explicitly for the first time: *When Johnny Comes Marching Home* — the cell that has been transforming across all three movements. Its history (Civil War-era, contemporaneous with the Buffalo Soldier regiments), its racial DNA (triplet anacrusis at the intersection of European march tradition and African diasporic rhythmic logic), its transformation (geological → chemical → human). The reader can now hear the whole score in retrospect.

**The Framework Found**

- ST as methodology discovered through making, not imposed upon it. The URET cycle and four domains as what emerged from sustained practice — not a framework that preceded the work but one that the work produced.
- *Speculocultural Technopoiesis* named here as a scholarly contribution: what it is, what it offers to HCI, sonic interaction design, digital media, Black studies, research-creation methodology. The specific-to-universal argument made.
- *Material Synthesis Composition (MSC)*  named here as a practice contribution: relational material substrates (organic matter, inorganic data from cultural accumulations) positioned as primary compositional feed alongside human performers and AI/ML systems. This is what the three instruments collectively invented. It could not have been named before they were built.

**Both Keys at Once**

- The two temporalities held simultaneously, finally named together:
  - Geological/elemental: earth → water → body. Cosmological time. The time of becoming.
  - Historical/linear: past (colonial archive) → speculative future (Afrofuturist projection) → present (the living body, now).
  - These are not contradictory — they are polytonal. Both true in the same score.

- The dissertation as instrument: it was made, it carries a philosophy, it will be modified by future players. The instrument-making metaphor completes itself.

- What the score leaves open: the unresolved questions, the productive failures, the next ST cycle. Irresolution is not weakness — it is the condition of ongoing research-creation.

**For Future Performers**

- How to use ST as a methodology for one's own cultural practice: the transmissible procedures
- How to use MSC as a compositional approach: the documented workflows, annotated code, decision rationales, naming conventions
- The Somax2 integration as a model for AI co-creation grounded in culturally specific corpora
- The invitation: *take this instrument and tune it to your own cultural frequency*

**The Long Note**

- The LONG note of the motif — held. This is the Coda's final section, and it should be short. Not summary; resonance.
- The trombone story returns, transformed. The instrument you play was made by someone with a philosophy of sound. Now you know what philosophy this one carries: Black sonic epistemologies as computational logic, modification as epistemic act, specificity as the path to generalization, sound as the field of potential from which all of this emerged.
- Final image/sentence: an echo of the opening of the Composer's Note, but transformed by what has passed. Sound was here before the instrument was built. It is still here. It will be here when this instrument is set down and another one is picked up and tuned.

## ORCHESTRATION NOTES *(for the composer — not in the dissertation)*

- **The drone** throughout: sonic virtuality, the trombone story as first surface, the phrase "tools carry the intentions of their makers" returning transformed
- **Attacca or breath**: Ground → Hold probably needs a breath (the question that ends Ground opens Hold). Hold → Touch could be attacca — Touch interrupts.
- **Leitmotif arc**: subliminal (Ground) → emerging (Hold) → explicit (Touch) → named in full (Coda)
- **Increasing intimacy**: geological distance → oceanic witnessing → bodily participation. The reader is drawn in across the score.
- **Global theories appear when summoned**: Ihde lands in Ground (piezo as hermeneutic relation) and Touch (the instrument as hermeneutic relation). Barad lands in all three (each instrument as apparatus). Eshun/sonic fiction is the water beneath all three movements. They don't have a chapter — they have a presence.
- **Solo passages**: each movement should have at least one section that is almost monophonic — just the researcher, the material, the sound. The interruptions in Touch are the most explicit version of this. Ground's Deep Layer and Hold's Dissolution sections should have this quality too.
